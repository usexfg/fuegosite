## Commitment deposits (CDs)

<Warning>
  CD yield is variable and not guaranteed. Interest depends entirely on atomic swap volume on the Fuego network. During periods of low swap activity, yield may be very low or zero.
</Warning>

**Lock-up risk.** Your XFG is inaccessible until the maturity height. You cannot withdraw early. If you need the funds before maturity, your only option is to transfer the deposit to another party, typically at a discount.

**Interest rate risk.** You lock in a term but not a rate. If swap volume decreases significantly after you lock, you earn less than expected.

**Smart contract risk.** Commitment deposit logic is enforced by the Fuego consensus rules. A consensus bug could theoretically affect your deposit. Fuego's consensus rules are open source — review them if you are locking significant amounts.

## Atomic swaps

**Counterparty risk.** If your counterparty goes offline before completing the swap, you must wait for the timelock to expire before reclaiming your XFG. During this window, your funds are locked. This is not a loss — it is a delay.

**Timelock duration.** The timelock duration depends on the counterparty chain. ETH-side timelocks are longer than XMR-side due to confirmation requirements. Check the swap daemon documentation for your pair.

**No price guarantee.** There is no slippage protection in atomic swaps. You and the counterparty agree on a price off-chain. If the market moves between agreement and completion, the agreed price is still what executes.

## LP swap pool

<Warning>
  Providing liquidity to the LP pool carries impermanent loss risk. If the XFG price moves significantly while you are an LP, you may withdraw less XFG than you deposited, even after accounting for fee income.
</Warning>

**Impermanent loss.** This is inherent to AMM design. Large price movements against your position reduce your effective return. The 0.3% fee income may not offset significant price moves.

**Smart contract risk.** LP pool logic is enforced on-chain. Review the pool mechanics in developer documentation before committing large amounts.

## HEAT burns

<Danger>
  HEAT burns are permanent and irrevocable. XFG locked in a burn deposit cannot be withdrawn under any circumstances. There is no recovery path. No future protocol upgrade will unlock burn deposits.
</Danger>

**Permanent loss of principal.** You are permanently reducing your XFG balance. The only return is HEAT tokens via off-chain claiming — this is not guaranteed to offset the burned XFG value.

**COLDAO dependency.** HEAT token value depends on COLDAO adoption. COLDAO is an off-chain system — its mechanics and value are external to the Fuego protocol.

**Proof generation required.** To claim HEAT, you must generate a STARK proof using your wallet's deposit secret. If you lose your wallet file after burning, your HEAT claim is lost. Back up your wallet file before burning.

---

## General

**No reversals.** All Fuego transactions — sends, deposits, withdrawals, swaps, burns — are irreversible once confirmed. Double-check everything before broadcasting.

**Privacy is probabilistic.** Ring signatures hide your transaction in a set of decoys. This provides strong probabilistic privacy but is not absolute. Behavioral patterns (timing, amounts, on-chain interaction patterns) can reduce effective privacy.
