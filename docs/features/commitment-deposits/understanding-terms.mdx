## What is an epoch

An epoch is the fundamental time unit for CD accounting. At each epoch boundary, the fee pool distributes accumulated swap fees to all locked CDs.

| Network | Blocks per epoch | Approximate real time |
|---------|-----------------|----------------------|
| Mainnet | 900 | ~1 day |
| Testnet | 10 | ~5 minutes |

Fuego targets approximately 96-second block times. Epoch boundaries trigger fee distribution and interest accrual automatically — no action required from you.

## Available terms

When creating a deposit, you choose a term in epochs. Current supported values are:

| Term code | Epochs | Mainnet duration |
|-----------|--------|-----------------|
| `3` | ~90 | ~3 months |
| `12` | ~365 | ~12 months |

<Info>
  Terms run from 1 to 72 epochs at the protocol level. The simplewallet CLI exposes the two most practical options. Use the walletd RPC to specify exact epoch counts.
</Info>

## Maturity height

Your deposit unlocks at a specific block height, not a specific date:

```
maturity_height = creation_height + (term_epochs × blocks_per_epoch)
```

Example on mainnet:
- Created at block 100,000
- Term: 90 epochs × 900 blocks = 81,000 blocks
- Maturity at block 181,000

Block height is deterministic — no uncertainty about when maturity occurs.

## Locked until maturity

You cannot withdraw a deposit before its maturity height. Attempting to do so produces an error. If you need liquidity before maturity, your options are:

1. **Transfer the deposit** to another party who pays you for it. See [transferring deposits](/features/commitment-deposits/transferring-deposits).
2. Accept the wait.

There is no early-withdrawal penalty in the protocol sense — early withdrawal simply isn't possible.

## Choosing a term

Longer terms accumulate interest across more epochs, which generally produces more total yield assuming consistent swap volume. Shorter terms give you flexibility to react to changing conditions.

A common strategy is to start with a shorter term, evaluate the yield rate, then roll over into a longer term if conditions are favorable. [Rollover](/features/commitment-deposits/compound-interest) lets you do this atomically while compounding your earned interest.
