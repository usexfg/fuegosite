## What CD transfer enables

Unlike traditional certificates of deposit, a Fuego CD can be transferred to another address before it matures. The new owner receives the deposit with its original maturity height intact — they can withdraw or roll over it once it matures.

This creates a secondary market: if you need liquidity before maturity, you can find a buyer and transfer at a negotiated price.

## How it works on-chain

Transfer uses `TransactionInputCommitmentTransfer`, a distinct transaction type that does not require the deposit to be mature. The original owner signs a transfer that reassigns the commitment to a new key controlled by the recipient's wallet.

The transfer does not claim any interest — interest accrues to whoever holds the deposit at maturity.

## Transfer process

<Steps>
  <Step title="Agree on a price off-chain">
    There is no on-chain order book. Find a buyer through community channels and agree on an XFG price for the deposit. The buyer typically pays a discount to face value to compensate for waiting until maturity.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Buyer sends payment">
    The buyer sends the agreed XFG amount to your wallet in a standard transaction. Confirm receipt before proceeding.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Transfer the deposit">
    Use the walletd RPC to transfer the commitment to the buyer's address:

    ```bash
    curl -X POST http://127.0.0.1:28080/transfer_deposit \
      -H 'Content-Type: application/json' \
      -d '{
        "deposit_id": "0",
        "recipient_address": "fire..."
      }'
    ```
  </Step>
  <Step title="Buyer verifies receipt">
    The buyer checks their wallet with `list_cold` to confirm the deposit appears under their control.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Pricing a transfer

There is no protocol-enforced pricing. Common approaches:

- **Discount to face value**: `deposit_amount × (remaining_term / total_term)` — buyer pays less for more time remaining
- **Yield-adjusted**: account for expected interest the buyer will receive at maturity
- **Market rate**: whatever the two parties agree on

## Privacy in transfers

The transfer is recorded on-chain as a new commitment reassignment. Amounts remain hidden — an observer sees that a transfer occurred but not how much XFG it represents or what price was paid off-chain.

## Risks for buyers

<Warning>
  When buying a transferred CD, you are trusting that the seller has not already used the deposit secret in another transaction. Verify the deposit appears in `list_cold` in your wallet before releasing payment.
</Warning>

The deposit amount and maturity height are fixed — they cannot be modified after creation. What you see in `cold_info` is exactly what you will receive at maturity.
