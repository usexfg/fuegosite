## Deposit into the pool

Use the walletd RPC to deposit liquidity:

```bash
curl -X POST http://127.0.0.1:28080/lp_deposit \
  -H 'Content-Type: application/json' \
  -d '{
    "amount": 100000000000,
    "address": "fire..."
  }'
```

`amount` is in atomic units (1 XFG = 1,000,000,000).

The daemon returns a transaction hash confirming your deposit. Your LP share is tracked on-chain using a ZK commitment that hides your exact amount.

## Check your LP share

```bash
curl -X POST http://127.0.0.1:28080/get_lp_position \
  -H 'Content-Type: application/json' \
  -d '{}'
```

Returns your current LP share and estimated pending fees.

## Withdraw liquidity

```bash
curl -X POST http://127.0.0.1:28080/lp_withdraw \
  -H 'Content-Type: application/json' \
  -d '{"amount": 100000000000}'
```

You receive your principal plus accumulated fees. If the pool price has moved since you deposited, your returned XFG amount may differ from your deposit (impermanent loss).

## Impermanent loss

<Warning>
  If the XFG price relative to other assets moves significantly while you are an LP, you may receive less XFG than you deposited when you withdraw. This is impermanent loss — it is inherent to AMM design, not a bug. The 0.3% fee income may or may not offset it depending on trade volume and price movement.
</Warning>

Impermanent loss is minimized when prices are stable. High-volume, low-volatility periods are most favorable for LPs.

## When to provide liquidity

Providing liquidity is most profitable when:
- Swap volume is high (more fees collected)
- Price is stable (less impermanent loss)
- The pool depth is low relative to demand (your share is larger)
